# Customer Segments: DecisionBetter
*Written: March 2026 | Phase 0*

---

## Segment 1: The Curious Owner ("I know I should be using AI but don't know where to start")

**Demographics / Firmographics**
- Business owner/founder, 35–55 years old
- SMB: 5–30 employees
- Industries: retail, hospitality, professional services, trades, e-commerce
- Revenue: AUD $500K–$5M
- Location: Sydney metro (near-term), broader NSW/national (future)
- Tech literacy: low to moderate (uses standard business software, not technical)

**Needs**
- Reassurance that AI is accessible to non-technical people
- Practical, visible quick wins that justify the investment
- Someone to do the hard thinking and translation work
- Proof that this isn't a waste of money

**Willingness to Pay**
- Workshop (half/full day): AUD $2,000–$4,000
- DFY project: AUD $5,000–$15,000 (budget-constrained end of market)
- Monthly retainer: AUD $500–$1,500 (if value is clear)

**Current Alternatives**
- YouTube tutorials, LinkedIn AI content (free, low effort, low results)
- Generic AI tools they've "tried" but abandoned
- Word-of-mouth advice from peers

**Switching Costs**
- Low: no deep incumbent relationship
- Emotional: fear of looking foolish or wasting money is a barrier to even trying

**Value Proposition for This Segment**
"We come to your business, use your actual data and processes, and show you and your team exactly how AI fits your workflow — with an outcome guarantee. No tech jargon, no generic demos."

**TAM estimate (bottom-up)**
- ~2.5M SMBs in Australia; ~63% of Sydney metro SMBs = ~150K businesses in Sydney
- ~37% have "tried AI" but only 5% are fully enabled → ~47K in Sydney in "tried but stuck" zone
- Target sub-segment (5–30 employees, $500K+ revenue): ~15% of total SMBs = ~7,000 in Sydney
- At 1 workshop/month, this segment is essentially infinite relative to solo capacity

## Segment 2: The Operational Leader ("We're already using AI tools but not getting real value")

**Demographics / Firmographics**
- Operations Manager, GM, or founder in a growing SMB
- 20–100 employees
- Industries: professional services (accounting, law, real estate), healthcare administration, logistics, financial services
- Revenue: AUD $2M–$20M
- Tech literacy: moderate (uses SaaS tools, may have tried Copilot/ChatGPT)

**Needs**
- Systematic AI implementation across multiple workflows (not one-off fix)
- Skills transfer to their team (not just "build it for me")
- Clear ROI measurement
- Ongoing optimization as AI tools evolve

**Willingness to Pay**
- Workshop/training program: AUD $5,000–$15,000
- DFY project: AUD $20,000–$60,000
- Monthly retainer: AUD $2,000–$5,000

**Current Alternatives**
- Enterprise consultancies (too expensive, over-engineered for their needs)
- Internal IT/digital transformation staff (expensive, rare)
- Generic online courses for teams (no customization to their workflow)
- DIY experimentation (slow, inconsistent)

**Switching Costs**
- Moderate: some investment in existing tools, resistant to adding yet another vendor
- High emotional switching cost if they've already had a bad AI consultant experience

**Value Proposition for This Segment**
"We map your existing workflows, identify the highest-ROI AI automation opportunities, implement them, and train your team to own the tools — so you're not dependent on us forever."

**TAM estimate (bottom-up)**
- ~50,000 SMBs in Australia with 20–100 employees
- ~40% in priority industries (professional services, healthcare, logistics)
- ~20,000 addressable; high-value segment: $20K–$60K per engagement
- At even 0.5% penetration: 100 clients × $30K avg = $3M revenue potential

## Segment 3: The Workshop Buyer ("I want to upskill my team in AI")

**Demographics / Firmographics**
- HR Manager, L&D Manager, or business owner with 10–50 employees
- Any industry with administrative/knowledge workers
- Revenue: AUD $1M–$10M
- Tech literacy: varies across team

**Needs**
- Structured team training (not self-paced online courses)
- Content adapted to their business context (not generic scenarios)
- Actionable outputs their team can use immediately
- Credibility — something they can show the board or leadership

**Willingness to Pay**
- Half-day workshop: AUD $2,500–$5,000
- Full-day workshop: AUD $4,000–$8,000
- Multi-session program (6 weeks): AUD $15,000–$30,000

**Current Alternatives**
- PM-Partners, RMIT Online, LinkedIn Learning for Teams (generic, not tailored)
- Internal IT team running informal lunch-and-learns (low quality)
- Big 4 training programs (too expensive, too corporate)

**Switching Costs**
- Low: training is episodic, not deeply embedded
- Strong desire for local, in-person delivery (not just online) is a filter

**Value Proposition for This Segment**
"A hands-on workshop using your own business scenarios and data — your team leaves with AI tools they can use on Monday morning, not theory they forget by Friday."

## Segment 4: The Skeptic ("I don't think AI is for businesses like mine")

**Demographics / Firmographics**
- Business owner in traditional industries: trades, construction, hospitality, agriculture
- 5–20 employees
- Revenue: AUD $500K–$3M
- Tech literacy: low; predominantly paper/phone-based operations

**Needs**
- Proof of concept in their specific industry
- Low-risk entry point (won't spend $5K on something they don't believe in)
- Plain-English ROI case before committing

**Willingness to Pay**
- Entry workshop: AUD $500–$1,500 (strong price sensitivity)
- DFY if convinced: AUD $5,000–$20,000
- Retainer: unlikely initially

**Current Alternatives**
- Nothing (the alternative is "do nothing")
- Peers' anecdotal advice

**Switching Costs**
- Very low financial switching cost but high psychological cost (pride, skepticism)

**Value Proposition for This Segment**
"A 3-hour session to show you exactly what AI can do for [industry] businesses like yours — or your money back."

**Strategic note**: This segment has enormous TAM (the "91% not using AI" market) but is the hardest and most expensive to convert. Likely a **Tier 3 priority** for near-term revenue, with potential as a scalable product if the right low-cost entry product is built.

## Segment Priority for Near-Term Focus

| Priority | Segment | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Operational Leader (Segment 2) | Highest LTV, most likely to convert to retainer, pays enough to sustain solo business |
| 2 | Workshop Buyer (Segment 3) | Easy to close, repeatable product, best path to referrals |
| 3 | Curious Owner (Segment 1) | Large TAM, requires most education selling, lower average deal value |
| 4 | Skeptic (Segment 4) | Cheapest entry, hardest conversion, lowest near-term ROI |
