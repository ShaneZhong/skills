# Business Context

## What does the business do?

DecisionBetter (decisionbetter.com) provides AI training and implementation services for Australian SMBs. The core positioning is "AI as a coworker" — integrating AI into existing business workflows rather than selling standalone tools.

Two primary offerings:
1. **AI Workshop** — Hands-on, in-person training tailored to each client's business. Half-day or full-day sessions using real client data and workflows. Non-technical, plain English approach. Outcome guarantee: measurable time savings or continued support.
2. **Done For You** — Full-service: map workflows, build custom AI systems, handle integration, ongoing support. No lock-in contracts.

Revenue model: Service fees (workshop fees + done-for-you project fees). No recurring SaaS revenue yet.

## Current state

- **Stage**: Early revenue / pre-scale
- **Team size**: 1 (solo founder). Key roles missing: sales/BD, delivery support, marketing
- **Revenue**: Early stage — exact figures not public
- **Runway/budget**: Bootstrapped, lean operation. Limited budget for paid acquisition or hiring.
- **Existing customers**: Small number of early clients. Acquired through network and direct outreach.

## Market

- **Who are the competitors?**
  - Direct: Other AI consultants targeting Australian SMBs (Wilson AI, various freelancers on Clutch.co, local boutique consultancies)
  - Indirect: DIY learning (YouTube, courses), enterprise consultancies (Decision Inc., Protiviti, Deloitte) pricing down-market, SaaS tools with built-in AI (Canva, HubSpot, etc. making AI "easy enough" that consulting isn't needed)
- **What's your edge?** Hands-on, non-technical approach. Uses real client data in workshops (not generic demos). Outcome guarantee. Solo founder = flexible, personal service.
- **Market trend**: Growing rapidly. Australian AI market expected to grow 26.25% annually through 2031. But: 91% of SMBs still not AI-enabled (Deloitte 2025). Only 23-25% of Australian SMEs currently using AI. Massive addressable market, but also increasing competition as more consultants enter the space.

## Capabilities and team strengths

- **What is the team good at?** Deep AI/ML technical knowledge, hands-on implementation experience, ability to translate complex AI concepts to non-technical business owners, workshop facilitation
- **What are the key skill gaps?** Sales/business development (currently founder-led), marketing/content creation at scale, delivery capacity (can only serve limited clients as solo operator), finance/pricing strategy
- **Culture**: Technical, fast-moving, experimental
- **Past change efforts**: New business — no pivots yet

## Constraints

- **What can't you do?** Can't serve many clients simultaneously (capacity of 1). Limited marketing budget. Can't compete on price with offshore/automated solutions. Geographic constraint (Sydney-based, in-person workshops).
- **What have you already tried?** Website launched, initial client outreach, positioned around "AI coworker" messaging.

## Goals

- **Primary goal**: Build a sustainable revenue stream and establish market position in Australian SMB AI consulting within 6-12 months.
- **Secondary goal**: Develop scalable offerings that reduce dependency on founder's direct time.
- **What are you optimizing for?** Revenue growth + building a repeatable, scalable business model (not just trading time for money).

## Anything else the strategist should know?

- The 91% non-adoption stat suggests a massive awareness/education gap — most SMBs don't know what AI can do for them
- Workshop completion rate target of 85%+ vs industry 5-15% suggests strong differentiation in delivery quality
- The "Done For You" service is high-touch and doesn't scale without hiring
- No public pricing — discovery call model suggests custom/premium positioning
