# Research: Government Grants + Cohort/Group Models
*Iteration 1 | March 2026*

---

## 1. Government Grant Tailwinds for AI Consultants

### AI Adopt Program [Hard data — business.gov.au]
- Funding: $3M–$5M per centre over 4 years (up to 50% of eligible expenditure)
- Purpose: Establish AI Adopt Centres to help SMEs in international/interstate trade adopt AI
- Status: Grant recipients already announced (program is active)
- Relevance for DecisionBetter: Too large for a solo operator to apply directly. However, DecisionBetter could potentially sub-contract to an AI Adopt Centre grantee as a delivery partner.

### ASBAS Digital Solutions Round 3 [Hard data — business.gov.au]
- Total funding: AUD $25.136M over 5 years (2025-26 to 2029-30)
- Purpose: Service providers deliver low-cost digital advisory services to small businesses, now explicitly including AI adoption and cybersecurity
- Eligible applicants: Incorporated entities, registered charities, ABN + GST registered trading corporations
- Applications closed: 5 December 2025 (CLOSED — this round is past application deadline)
- Geographic service areas: Multiple; separate application per area
- **Implication**: Round 3 is closed. However, successful ASBAS providers will be delivering AI advisory services — DecisionBetter could partner with an ASBAS provider as a specialist subcontractor/advisor.

### SA AI Capability Pilot Program [Hard data — business.sa.gov.au]
- Funding: Up to AUD $370,000 over 18 months (2025–2027)
- For: Delivery partners designing AI capability support for SA small businesses
- **Implication**: NSW-focused DecisionBetter cannot directly benefit, but there may be equivalent NSW programs emerging.

### Australian Government AI Investment [Informed opinion — InnoApps]
- Total government AI commitment: AUD $3.99B across multiple programs through 2025
- Primary sectors: healthcare, transport, clean energy, agriculture, defence

### Key finding for strategy
The government grant landscape is real but largely inaccessible to a solo operator as a direct applicant. The opportunity is **indirect**: becoming a registered subcontractor or delivery partner to ASBAS/AI Adopt Centre grantees who need specialist AI workshop and implementation capability to deliver their programs.

**Counter-research finding**: The ASBAS program has had delivery challenges historically (one site shows "Digital Solutions is not currently delivering the ASBAS Service" without explanation). Government programs add reporting overhead and constrained pricing. The bureaucratic burden could consume more time than the revenue justifies for a solo operator.

## 2. Cohort and Group Workshop Models

### What's available in AU market
- AI Consulting Group (aiconsultinggroup.com.au): 1–3 day education and strategy workshops for organisations
- Matrix AI: Tailored training in-person, 1:1, or "exclusive group settings"
- AusBiz Consulting: AI bootcamps for teams

### The cohort opportunity for solo operators
A **cohort-based group program** (e.g., 6–8 SMB owners going through an AI implementation program together) offers significant leverage:
- One instructor serves 6–8 clients simultaneously
- Peer learning adds value (participants teach each other)
- Community adds retention (ongoing cohort relationships)
- Revenue multiplier: 8 clients × $3,000 = $24,000 for the same delivery as 1 client at $3,000

### Cohort model risks (counter-research)
- Cohort fatigue is a known risk in online education (people drop out)
- In-person cohort logistics are complex (scheduling 6–8 SMB owners simultaneously)
- "Generic" feel of group programs conflicts with DecisionBetter's "real client data" differentiation
- Requires minimum cohort size to launch (dependency on filling spots)

### Best-fit cohort model for DecisionBetter
A **"peer learning" format** where participants bring their own business problems and learn from each other's AI implementations could preserve the "real data" differentiation while enabling scale. Industry-specific cohorts (e.g., "AI for Accountants") would attract a focused group with similar workflows.
