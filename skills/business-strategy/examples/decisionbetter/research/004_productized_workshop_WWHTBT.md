# Research: Productized Workshop Stress Test (WWHTBT)
*Iteration 4 | March 2026*

---

## What Would Have to Be True (WWHTBT) for Option A to Work

Option A (Productized Workshop Ladder) is currently Tier 1 at composite 4.36. This iteration stress-tests its key assumptions.

---

## Condition 1: SMB buyers will act on published pricing without a discovery call

**Evidence FOR** [Informed opinion — consultingsuccess.com, assembly.com]:
- 44% of consultants now offer productized services
- Transparent pricing increases conversion by eliminating scope/price uncertainty
- Successful models give visitors two choices: "buy now" or "book a call if you need more info"
- One-day workshops for 20 people at $497/person (~$10K total) are commercially validated at the group level

**Evidence AGAINST** [Informed opinion — getzendo.io, lodestaragencyconsulting.com]:
- "Slapping prices on existing services" without redesigning the offer leads to confused prospects and scope creep
- B2B consulting buyers often want to talk to someone before committing — especially for custom/in-person services
- Pure self-serve pricing works for sub-$1K offers; for $3K–$5K workshop, most buyers still want one qualifying conversation

**Assessment**: Partial validation. The productized pricing model works best when:
- Price point is clearly within the buyer's "no-brainer" zone (sub-$1,500) for self-serve OR
- Higher prices are paired with a very clear outcomes specification (what you get, what changes, the guarantee)
- Decision: A **hybrid approach** (visible prices + a 15-minute qualifying call) is likely optimal at the $3K–$5K price point. Full self-serve better suited to the Budget Taster (Option K at ~$1K).

**Status**: Partially validated. The pricing-visible-online model reduces friction. Full no-call self-serve at $4.5K unlikely. Hybrid model recommended.

---

## Condition 2: Current workshop delivery quality is reproducible in a fixed-scope format

**Evidence FOR**:
- DecisionBetter already delivers workshops with "85%+ completion rate" (vs. industry 5–15%) — strong signal that delivery quality is high
- The "real client data" approach is a method, not a variable — can be systematized
- Many consulting firms have successfully created fixed-scope intensive workshops that use client-provided data in a structured format

**Evidence AGAINST**:
- "Fixed scope" creates risk of under-delivering for clients with complex or unusual workflows
- The outcome guarantee requires that the workshop actually produces "measurable time savings" — hard to guarantee with fixed time regardless of client context
- Scope creep is a known risk in productized consulting engagements

**Assessment**: Largely validated. The solution: design the fixed scope with a "diagnostic intake form" that clients complete before the workshop (30 minutes online), allowing Shane to customise the session focus without extending the sales cycle. This preserves the "real data" experience while keeping delivery predictable.

**Status**: Validated with mitigation (diagnostic intake form).

---

## Condition 3: Pricing at AUD $2,500–$5,000 is within SMB budget comfort zone

**Evidence FOR** [Hard data — market research]:
- AU market rate for full-day AI workshop is AUD $3K–$6K/day
- AIWise charges $990 for a discovery session and $3K+ for implementations
- SMB budgets for technology/consulting: AUD $5K–$15K typical for professional services (per BizCover survey data)
- 80% of AU SMBs are using AI or planning to adopt — significant "believe in AI" segment

**Evidence AGAINST**:
- SMBs with <10 employees (micro businesses) are price-sensitive; $5K is a significant decision
- Segment 1 (Curious Owner) likely needs more ROI justification before committing $5K
- The "no public pricing" approach of competitors may signal that high-ticket deals require relationship selling

**Assessment**: Validated for Segments 2 and 3 (Operational Leader, Workshop Buyer). Requires Budget Taster (Option K) as an entry point for Segment 1. Full productized ladder should be: $1,000 Taster → $4,500 Workshop → $30K DFY — each step self-justifies the next.

**Status**: Validated for target segments; requires taster product for lower-end buyers.

---

## Condition 4: Productization doesn't commoditise DecisionBetter's positioning

**Evidence FOR** [Informed opinion]:
- The risk of commoditisation only applies when productization is "slapping prices on services"
- True differentiation comes from: the outcome guarantee, the "real client data" methodology, and the specific outcomes articulated
- A workshop named "AI Workflow Sprint: 12 hours over 2 days, guaranteed 3 identified workflow automations worth $X in time savings" is NOT a commodity — it's a specific promise
- 44% of consultants now productize; the ones that succeed differentiate on outcomes, not just process

**Evidence AGAINST** [Informed opinion — lodestaragencyconsulting.com]:
- Bundling deliverables (not outcomes) turns you into a "vendor with a menu"
- Competing purely on price after productizing leads to race to bottom
- Without strong brand/authority, price becomes the primary differentiator

**Assessment**: Validated IF the productization is outcome-led (specific time savings promised, not just "a workshop"). DecisionBetter's outcome guarantee is the key differentiator that prevents commoditisation. The guarantee is the product; the workshop is the delivery mechanism.

**Status**: Validated with condition: products must be named around outcomes, not deliverables.

---

## Overall WWHTBT Assessment for Option A

| Condition | Status | Action Needed |
|---|---|---|
| SMBs act on published pricing | Partially validated — hybrid model needed | Design: visible price + 15-min qualifying call (not full discovery) |
| Fixed scope maintains quality | Validated with mitigation | Design: diagnostic intake form before each workshop |
| $2,500–$5,000 is in budget | Validated for Segments 2/3; weak for Segment 1 | Add Budget Taster (Option K) as bottom of ladder |
| Productization doesn't commoditise | Validated if outcome-led | Name products around guaranteed outcomes, not deliverables |

**Conclusion**: Option A remains Tier 1. The stress test has refined the implementation approach rather than undermining the case. The integration with Option K (Budget Taster) is now a recommended interaction.
