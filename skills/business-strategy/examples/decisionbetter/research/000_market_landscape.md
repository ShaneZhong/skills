## 2. SMB AI Adoption: Current State

**Adoption rates vary significantly by survey methodology** [Informed opinion — multiple sources]:
- Dept. of Industry (Q1 2025): ~one-third of SMEs have adopted AI
- National AI Centre Tracker (400 SMEs/month via Fifth Quadrant): 37% adoption
- Deloitte 2025: two-thirds of SMBs using AI (broader definition of "using")
- BizCover 2025: 80% using or planning to adopt

**Key gap**: While adoption is spreading, **only 5% of surveyed SMBs are "fully enabled"** to realise AI's potential [Informed opinion — Scalesuite.com.au]. Most are operating at basic/surface-level usage — a massive opportunity for implementation consulting.

**Industry breakdown** [Hard data — Dept. of Industry Q1 2025]:
- Retail trade, health/education: leading sectors
- Marketing/consulting/ICT: highest intent (~91% use or plan to use)
- Hospitality, professional services: close behind
- 5 of 7 industries surveyed show >80% adoption or intent

**Geographic note**: Sydney-based consultants have access to Australia's largest SMB concentration.

## 3. Why SMB AI Projects Fail

This matters for positioning — DecisionBetter's differentiation should directly address failure causes. [Anecdotal/Informed — multiple sources including MIT, LeverageAI.com.au, HBR]:

1. **Poor data quality** — 43% of failed initiatives (European SMBs, analogous to AU)
2. **Misalignment between AI capabilities and business goals** — "AI for AI's sake"
3. **Organisational unreadiness** — no change management, skills transfer, or ownership
4. **Over-technical delivery** — consultants build demos that don't survive production
5. **Lack of consistent in-house expertise** after project-based consultants leave (AU-specific)
6. **MIT 2025 estimate**: 95% of generative AI pilots fail; 42% of initiatives scrapped in 2025 (up from 17% in 2024)

Source: https://leverageai.com.au/the-seven-deadly-mistakes-why-most-smb-ai-projects-are-designed-to-fail-and-how-to-fix-it-2/

**DecisionBetter relevance**: The "outcome guarantee" and "AI coworker" (not just tools) positioning directly targets failure causes #3 and #4. This is a meaningful differentiator if communicated clearly.

## 5. Competitive Landscape Overview

**Tier 1 — Established AU boutiques targeting SMBs:**
- Wilson AI (Melbourne, founded 2018, ~$362K revenue est.)
- AIWise (discovery from $990, implementations from $3K)
- Sunburnt AI (Australian-owned, enterprise-grade infrastructure focus)
- Arcadian Digital (Melbourne, digital agency with AI module)

**Tier 2 — National/enterprise firms pricing into SMB:**
- Decision Inc., Protiviti, Deloitte (typically out of reach for small SMBs on price)
- Matrix AI (NZ + AU)

**Tier 3 — Indirect competitors:**
- DIY: YouTube, Coursera, LinkedIn Learning
- SaaS with embedded AI: HubSpot AI, Canva, Microsoft Copilot (reducing need for consulting)
- Offshore consultants on Clutch.co, Upwork (price competition)
- PM-Partners (training, not implementation)

**Fragmented market**: No dominant national SMB AI consulting brand. Top firms on Clutch AU have small review counts (indicating limited scale). This is a land-grab opportunity.

## 6. Market Trends

**Trend 1: From "awareness" to "implementation" urgency** [Informed opinion]
The market is moving from "should we use AI?" to "we know we need to, but how?" — shifting demand from education workshops toward implementation support.

**Trend 2: AI agents and automation overtaking simple chatbots** [Informed opinion]
The AI conversation has shifted from "AI for content" to "AI agents for business processes" — multi-step workflows, voice AI, document processing. Higher-value, stickier implementations.

**Trend 3: Government support and procurement tailwinds** [Hard data — AU Government]
Commonwealth Procurement Rules now mandate SME quotas. Possible grant programs for AI adoption.

**Trend 4: Competitive crowding beginning** [Anecdotal]
Multiple new entrants (Sunburnt AI, AIWise, etc.) appearing in the market. Window for early positioning is narrowing but not closed. The market is still fragmented.

**Trend 5: DIY displacement risk** [Hard data — product adoption]
Microsoft Copilot, Google Workspace AI, HubSpot AI, Canva AI — enterprise tools are making some "AI adoption" trivially easy without consultants. The consulting market may bifurcate: trivial use cases commoditised, complex workflow integration remains consultant-territory.

---

## 7. Key Insight for Strategy

**The 5% fully-enabled gap is the real market**: 37% of SMBs have "adopted AI" but only 5% are fully enabled. The other ~32% are ripe for upgrade/deepening services. These are warm leads who already believe in AI — they just need better implementation. This is a far easier sale than convincing skeptics.
