# Research: Iteration 006 — Strategic Fork Analysis: Path A vs Path B
*Date: March 2026*

---

## Context

The diagnosis.md frames the core strategic question as: "Build acquisition system around current model (Path A) OR productize first (Path B)?"

Owner comment mandates: "Do a strategy analysis on both options." This file documents the WWHTBT analysis for both paths.

---

## Path A: Build Acquisition System First

**Definition**: Keep current bespoke workshop + DFY offerings. Invest effort in building a systematic lead generation engine — referral partners, direct outreach, speaking, private community — to generate a steady pipeline of clients who then go through the existing discovery/scoping process.

### WWHTBT for Path A

**Condition A1**: The current offering is compelling enough that, once found, prospects convert at reasonable rates.
- Status: **Partially validated.** Shane has existing clients acquired through network. But no conversion rate data on cold or warm leads.
- Risk: If the conversion rate on cold/warm leads is <5%, the acquisition system will require enormous volume to generate revenue.

**Condition A2**: Shane has enough capacity to serve the clients an acquisition system would generate.
- Status: **Likely NOT MET.** Current capacity ~3-4 clients/month. An effective acquisition system generating 5-8 leads/month with 30-50% conversion = 2-4 new clients/month = at or near capacity immediately.
- Risk: The acquisition system works, then generates a capacity crisis within 90 days.

**Condition A3**: Prospects can self-select the right engagement type without a lengthy scoping process.
- Status: **Unvalidated.** Current model is discovery-call-first. Without a productized entry, every acquired lead requires Shane's time for scoping, which creates a funnel bottleneck.
- Risk: Acquisition system generates leads; scoping calls consume time; effective client capacity doesn't grow.

**Condition A4**: The business can sustain revenue during the 3-6 months it takes for an acquisition system to generate consistent flow.
- Status: **Unknown.** Bootstrapped, no public revenue data. If existing clients provide some revenue, this may be viable.

### Path A Failure Modes
1. Acquisition system generates leads faster than can be served → Shane forced to turn away business or rush delivery
2. Without productization, every inbound lead requires bespoke scoping → high time cost per lead even before closing
3. The acquisition channels don't compound — speaking engagements are episodic, referrals require ongoing partner management
4. Shane's personal time is split between marketing/BD and delivery → quality drops in both

### Path A — 6-Month Outcome (Base Case)
- 3-5 referral partners established
- 10-20 speaking-sourced leads per quarter
- 4-6 new clients closed
- Revenue: AUD $40K-$80K additional (4 workshops + 2 DFY)
- Business is growing but fully founder-dependent; no scalable system

### Path A — 18-Month Outcome
- Strong network established; referral flow consistent
- Revenue ceiling: ~$200K-$250K (10-12 DFY projects + workshops)
- Hard capacity ceiling hit; need to hire or raise prices
- No self-serve element; business requires Shane for every touchpoint

## Path B: Productize First

**Definition**: Before investing in acquisition, redesign the offering into fixed-scope, fixed-price products with visible pricing, intake forms, and a clear scope. Then build acquisition channels pointing at specific, purchasable products.

### WWHTBT for Path B

**Condition B1**: Fixed-scope products can be designed that are specific enough to deliver consistently but flexible enough to cover most client situations.
- Status: **Validated (iteration 004 WWHTBT).** Diagnostic intake form + outcome-led product definitions + hybrid model ($15-min qualifying call for $4.5K+ products) resolves this.

**Condition B2**: Publishing prices does not materially reduce perceived value or enable competitors to undercut.
- Status: **Validated.** AU market data shows AI Avenue, AIWise already publish prices. Transparent pricing increases trust with SMB buyers. Competitors are not differentiating on price — they're differentiating on approach.

**Condition B3**: Productization creates an enabling layer that makes ALL subsequent acquisition channels more efficient.
- Status: **High confidence.** Without a specific product to point to, speaking CTAs ("come to my website"), referral partner conversations ("send me clients"), and direct outreach ("let's have a call") all require a secondary step. A specific product ("book the AI Workflow Sprint for $2,500") converts in ONE step.

**Condition B4**: The time investment in productization (2-4 weeks) is justified before starting acquisition.
- Status: **Validated.** The productization sprint is a one-time ~30-hour investment. Every acquisition channel benefits permanently. ROI of this foundation work is near-infinite.

### Path B Failure Modes
1. Productized offer doesn't attract buyers even after acquisition investment (offer-market fit failure)
2. Shane customises every engagement despite having "fixed" products → no real productization
3. The time spent on productization delays revenue-generating work

### Path B — 6-Month Outcome (Base Case)
- Products launched week 4
- 3 workshops delivered with case studies documented by week 10
- Acquisition channels activated by week 8
- Revenue: similar to Path A near-term but with better conversion efficiency
- Business has a self-serve entry layer; not every lead requires Shane's time immediately

### Path B — 18-Month Outcome
- Self-serve lower tier operates with minimal Shane involvement
- Case studies and reviews enabling higher conversion rates
- Foundation for AI-delivered components (long-term vision)
- Revenue ceiling same as Path A but with lower time-per-dollar

---

## Head-to-Head: Path A vs Path B

| Dimension | Path A (Acquisition First) | Path B (Productize First) |
|---|---|---|
| Time to first incremental revenue | Faster (start outreach now) | Slower (2-4 week delay for productization) |
| Revenue per hour of Shane's time (6 months) | Lower (bespoke scoping eats time) | Higher (products reduce scoping friction) |
| Scalability (18 months) | Hard ceiling without productization | Opens path to self-serve, automation |
| Risk if demand generation works | Immediate capacity crisis | Capacity crisis still possible but delayed by intake efficiency |
| Dependency on Shane | Complete (every step requires Shane) | High near-term, lower over time |
| Options it opens | Few (same bespoke model, more clients) | Many (DFY upsell, retainer, AI delivery, cohort) |
| Required before stealth channels work | Stealth channels work immediately | Need to spend 2-4 weeks first |

**Verdict**: Path B first, then Path A. The 2-4 week productization investment is justified because:
1. Every subsequent acquisition channel is permanently more efficient
2. Path A without productization creates a bespoke-scoping bottleneck that doesn't scale
3. Path B does not prevent Path A — once products exist, acquisition channels are activated simultaneously
4. The AI-delivered training long-term vision REQUIRES a productized foundation (you cannot AI-deliver bespoke consulting)

**Sequencing**: Productize (weeks 1-4) → Social proof sprint (weeks 5-10: 3 workshops, case studies, reviews) → Activate acquisition channels (weeks 8+, starting stealth-compatible ones)

**Can they be sequenced OR run simultaneously?**
- NOT simultaneously: running acquisition before productization forces Shane to scope every inbound lead, consuming the time needed for productization
- SEQUENCED with overlap: Start productization in week 1. In week 2, start activating warm network (stealth-compatible). By week 4, full acquisition system launch.
