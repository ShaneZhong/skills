## 2. Value-Based Pricing Framework

The correct way to price consulting products is not cost-plus or competitor-benchmarking — it is value capture: what fraction of the value delivered does the price represent?

### Value Delivered Per Workshop Tier

**AI Quick Wins Session ($997 — 3 hours)**
- Deliverable: Ranked list of 3-5 specific AI workflow opportunities for the specific business
- Conservative ROI: If just ONE opportunity is implemented, saving 2 hours/week for a team of 5 at $50/hour average: 2 hrs × 5 people × 52 weeks × $50 = AUD $26,000/year in recovered productivity
- Value capture ratio: $997 / $26,000 = **3.8% capture rate** — very low; strong value case
- Willingness to pay signal: AIWise's $990 entry session validates this price point in the AU market

**AI Workflow Sprint ($2,500 — half-day)**
- Deliverable: 2 workflows implemented + team trained + 30-day support
- Conservative ROI: 2 workflows × 1 hour/day saved × 250 working days × $50/hour = AUD $25,000/year
- Value capture ratio: $2,500 / $25,000 = **10% capture rate** — still conservative
- This is below AI Avenue's comparable team session ($5,500+)

**AI Team Transformation ($4,500 — full-day)**
- Deliverable: 3-4 workflows implemented + full team trained + 60-day support + outcome guarantee
- Conservative ROI: 3 workflows × 1.5 hours/day × 250 days × $50/hour = AUD $56,250/year
- Value capture ratio: $4,500 / $56,250 = **8% capture rate** — very low
- Comparable AI Avenue session: $5,500–$8,000+ for similar group coverage

**AI Adoption Program ($12,000 — multi-session)**
- Deliverable: 5+ workflows, monthly review, dedicated Slack, 4-6 weeks
- Conservative ROI: 5 workflows × $10,000 annualised productivity gain each = $50,000/year minimum
- Value capture ratio: $12,000 / $50,000 = **24% capture rate** — still reasonable for comprehensive program
- Comparable market: $15,000–$30,000 for multi-session programs (per customer_segments.md benchmarks)

## 3. Price Sensitivity Analysis by Segment

### Segment 1: Curious Owner (5-30 employees, $500K-$5M revenue)
- Typical decision-maker: Business owner personally
- Buying psychology: "Can I justify this to myself / my accountant?"
- Price sensitivity: HIGH below $1,000 (self-approval threshold), MODERATE for $1,000-$3,000 (mental threshold for "business investment"), HIGH above $5,000 (requires business case)
- **Sweet spot**: $997-$2,500 — within self-approval range for business investment
- Validation: AIWise's $990 entry point specifically designed for this segment

### Segment 2: Operational Leader (20-100 employees, $2M-$20M revenue)
- Typical decision-maker: GM or Operations Manager with budget authority
- Buying psychology: "Is this within my discretionary spend? Can I avoid board approval?"
- Price sensitivity: LOW below $5,000 (discretionary spend threshold for many businesses), MODERATE $5,000-$15,000, HIGH above $15,000 (triggers formal procurement)
- **Sweet spot**: $4,500-$12,000 — above PM-Partners commodity, below formal procurement threshold
- This segment's $4,500 workshop is effectively a loss leader for $20K-$60K DFY engagement

### Segment 3: Workshop Buyer (10-50 employees, $1M-$10M revenue, L&D focus)
- Typical decision-maker: HR/L&D Manager with training budget
- Buying psychology: "How does this compare to other training spend? Can I defend this to the CEO?"
- Annual L&D budget for 10-50 person firm: AUD $5,000-$50,000 typically
- Price sensitivity: MODERATE — they're used to spending on training but have budget limits
- **Sweet spot**: $4,500-$12,000 per program (comparable to other quality training programs)
- External validation: Corporate training market standard is $1,000-$3,000/person/day — for a 10-person team that is $10,000-$30,000/day at market rates. DecisionBetter's $4,500 full-day is dramatically underpriced for this segment.

## 4. Pricing Rationale: The Full Bottom-Up Case

### $997 AI Quick Wins Session
**Why $997, not $500 or $1,500?**
- $997 psychological: Below the $1,000 "four-figure" threshold. A deliberate pricing decision — the "$997" ending creates perception of precision and value.
- $500 is too cheap: Signals low quality; attracts bargain hunters who won't upsell; doesn't justify Shane's 3 hours + prep time at any reasonable rate ($150/hr × 5 hours = $750 cost base)
- $1,500 is viable but: Crosses the psychological "significant business investment" threshold for Segment 1 buyers; better for Segment 2/3 as the entry point
- Market validation: AIWise at $990 — close analogue, same price point, same AU SMB market
- Conclusion: $997 is the right price. Could potentially test $1,200-$1,500 as the market matures and reviews accumulate.

### $2,500 AI Workflow Sprint (half-day)
**Why $2,500, not $1,500 or $3,500?**
- AI Avenue charges $5,500 for a generic team workshop of similar duration. DecisionBetter offers more (real client data, outcome guarantee) for less.
- $1,500: Too close to the $997 taster — creates a confusing price ladder with insufficient value differentiation
- $2,500: Clear step up from $997; represents the "first serious commitment" tier; within Segment 1 discretionary spend for business owners $500K+ revenue
- $3,500: Approaches full-day pricing territory without full-day deliverables — buyers will question the value gap
- Value-based check: 10% capture of $25,000 annual ROI = $2,500 ✓
- Conclusion: $2,500 is appropriate. There is an argument for $3,000 as the market matures.

### $4,500 AI Team Transformation (full-day)
**Why $4,500, not $3,500 or $6,000?**
- $3,500: Creates insufficient price gap from $2,500 Sprint — buyers will question what an extra $1,000 gets them
- $4,500: Clear full-day premium; within Segment 2 discretionary spend threshold; 8% capture of $56,250 annual ROI
- $6,000: Enters the range where Segment 1 buyers require board/partner approval; still below AI Avenue but might reduce volume
- Psychological anchor: $4,500 feels like a significant investment but not prohibitive; $5,000 or $6,000 crosses a mental threshold for many SMB owners
- Competitor context: AI Avenue charges $5,500–$8,000+ for comparable full-day team sessions. Being below market here is a deliberate market penetration strategy while building reviews.
- Conclusion: $4,500 is appropriate for market entry. As reviews accumulate: test $5,500-$6,000 which is still below AI Avenue.

### $12,000 AI Adoption Program (multi-session)
**Why $12,000, not $8,000 or $15,000?**
- $8,000: Below the "multi-week program" psychological threshold; buyers expect to pay more for a 4-6 week structured engagement
- $12,000: Positions as a serious business transformation investment; below formal procurement threshold for most businesses; 24% capture of $50,000 ROI
- $15,000: Matches the lower end of market benchmarks (per customer_segments.md: $15,000-$30,000 for multi-session programs) but crosses the "triggers formal approval" threshold for Segment 1 buyers
- Phasing consideration: $12,000 entry price is designed to compete with DIY learning alternatives. As case studies accumulate and the program is refined, price should increase to $15,000-$20,000 at Year 2.
- Conclusion: $12,000 is a market-entry price, not a long-term price. It will need to be raised as the program matures.

## 5. Pricing Architecture: Is the Ladder Well-Designed?

| Tier | Price | Step-up % | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| Quick Wins Session | $997 | — | Entry; below $1K threshold; validates interest |
| Workflow Sprint | $2,500 | +151% | Clear step to "first real commitment" |
| Team Transformation | $4,500 | +80% | Full-day; team-wide; includes ongoing support |
| Adoption Program | $12,000 | +167% | Multi-week; full transformation; highest outcome guarantee |

**Assessment**: The pricing steps are large and well-differentiated. Each tier offers materially more value, justifying the significant price step. The ladder design is sound.

**One improvement**: Consider a $7,500-$8,000 tier between $4,500 and $12,000 for clients who want more than one day but not a 4-6 week program (e.g., a 3-session deep dive over 3 weeks). This would fill the gap and capture clients who are ready to move beyond a single day but not yet committed to a full program.

## 6. Price Sensitivity vs Competitor Positioning Summary

**DecisionBetter is priced BELOW comparable offerings at every tier:**
- $997 vs AIWise $990 (near-parity, slightly above — justified by more customisation)
- $2,500 for half-day vs AI Avenue $5,500+ for team session (less than half)
- $4,500 for full-day vs AI Avenue $5,500-$8,000+ (materially below)

**Strategic implication**: The current prices leave significant margin on the table. This is appropriate for a pre-review business — price penetration strategy to win first 10 clients. After 10 client case studies, prices should increase 20-40% across the board. The outcome guarantee justifies premium pricing once evidence accumulates.

**Summary**: The proposed pricing is well-structured, defensible, and slightly under-market for market entry. The rationale is: value-based pricing anchored to ROI (8-24% capture), calibrated against competitor benchmarks (below AI Avenue), with psychological price points ($997, $2,500, $4,500) that minimize approval friction for the target segments.
