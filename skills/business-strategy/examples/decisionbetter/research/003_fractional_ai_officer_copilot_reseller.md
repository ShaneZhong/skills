# Research: Fractional AI Officer Model + Microsoft Copilot Partner
*Iteration 3 | March 2026*

---

## 1. Fractional AI Officer / Fractional CTO Model

### Market evidence [Informed opinion — DigitalReference.co, AIWise, LinkedIn]
- Growing demand for fractional Chief AI Officers (CAIO) in Australia
- AIWise already offers "Fractional CTO" as a product (aiwise.com.au/services/fractional-cto/) — meaning a direct competitor has validated this market
- Fractional CTO market rate: AUD $6K–$10K/month depending on scope (globally benchmarked)
- US CAIO salary equivalent: $350K–$500K base; fractional delivers that value at 20–40% of cost

### Why it works for SMBs [Informed opinion]
- SMBs need strategic AI leadership but can't afford/justify a full-time AI executive
- Fractional model provides ongoing accountability (strategy, governance, KPIs) not just a one-time engagement
- Particularly valuable for businesses that have deployed some AI (the "5% fully-enabled" market) and need ongoing optimization

### Key distinction from retainer (Option D)
- Option D is an operational retainer (maintain, fix, expand existing AI systems)
- A Fractional AI Officer role is strategic (AI roadmap, AI governance, vendor selection, team capability building)
- The Fractional AI Officer commands higher rates (~$5K–$10K/month) than a pure support retainer

### Problems with the fractional model [Counter-research — Umbrex, 2GO Group]
- Engagement requires ongoing executive access (monthly leadership meetings), which is demanding
- Clients may expect "unlimited availability" despite part-time arrangement — boundary-setting required
- Churn happens when clients don't see clear strategic value vs. tactical support (the distinction must be made explicit)
- Solo operators running multiple fractional clients can become overextended — calendar risk is real

## 2. Microsoft Copilot Partner Program

### Opportunity [Hard data — Microsoft Partner Network, ARN Australia]
- Microsoft has structured a formal Copilot monetization framework for SMB-focused partners
- Partners can earn: co-selling revenue (nearly double growth for co-sell partners), Enterprise Customer Investment Fund increases (~20%), recurring revenue on Copilot seat renewals
- Copilot for Microsoft 365: AUD $30–$40/user/month (enterprise pricing; SMB pricing varies)
- Microsoft explicitly targeting SMB Copilot adoption through channel partners

### What a Copilot partner does [Informed opinion]
- Resell Microsoft 365 Copilot licences to SMB clients
- Deploy, configure, and train clients on Copilot
- Upsell to Copilot agents and custom automation
- Provide ongoing adoption support (the "implementation gap" that most Microsoft resellers don't fill)

### Relevance to DecisionBetter
- Microsoft Copilot is now the most widely adopted AI tool in Australian SMBs (bundled with Microsoft 365 which ~80% of AU businesses already use)
- Many SMBs have Copilot but aren't using it effectively — this is exactly DecisionBetter's "5% fully enabled" market
- Becoming a Microsoft partner provides: formal revenue stream (licence margins), qualified lead pipeline (Microsoft refers SMBs), and association credibility

### Risks [Counter-research]
- Microsoft partner programme requires CSP (Cloud Solution Provider) accreditation — involves compliance requirements and investment
- Copilot licence margins for SMB resellers are thin (~5–10%)
- Microsoft partnership doesn't mean Microsoft will actually refer leads — most partners self-generate
- Risk of becoming dependent on Microsoft's product roadmap and pricing decisions
