## Positioning

AIWise positions as an AI consulting and **automation agency** — emphasis on building automated systems rather than purely training/advisory. Their tagline-level message: "leverage artificial intelligence to streamline operations, enhance customer experiences, and drive growth."

Key differentiation: they lead with **specific AI products** (chatbots, agents, workflow automation, document processing, voice AI) rather than vague "AI strategy." This is a more product-like GTM than most boutique consultants.

---

## Target Customer

- SMBs and mid-market Australian businesses
- Buyers who want AI **built for them** (vs. trained to do it themselves)
- Likely appealing to businesses that prefer a done-for-you model

---

## Services

1. AI strategy and consulting
2. AI agents (multi-step business workflow automation)
3. AI chatbots
4. AI-powered workflows
5. Document processing automation
6. Voice AI
7. AI-driven personalisation
8. AI reporting and insights

**Service innovation note**: AIWise appears to be building toward a productized service model (named, defined outputs like "AI Email Handling Agent," "AI Reporting & Insights Agent") which is a scalability play.

---

## Pricing

**Discovery session**: AUD $990 + GST
- Includes: workflow process mapping, system integration review, next steps roadmap
- Duration: scoping session

**Implementations**: From AUD $3,000
- Final price based on complexity identified in scoping
- Most builds completed in 2–4 weeks

**Implication**: AIWise has a clear, low-friction entry point ($990 discovery) that converts to larger implementation projects. This is a well-structured funnel.

---

## Strengths

- Clear, transparent pricing (rare in this market — lowers buyer friction)
- Productized service names (AI agent "products") — easier to buy than bespoke consulting
- Fast delivery promise (2–4 weeks per build)
- Broader capability set than DecisionBetter (custom development)

---

## Weaknesses

- No focus on training/upskilling client teams (builds dependency, not capability)
- No visible outcome guarantee
- No reviews yet on GoodFirms or Clutch (new entrant)
- Does not appear to offer in-person/hands-on workshop format
- "Agency" positioning may feel less personal/trusted for relationship-oriented SMB buyers

---

## Recent Moves

No public announcements found. Website appears active and well-maintained as of March 2026. Presence on multiple directories suggests active marketing investment.

---

## Competitive Threat to DecisionBetter

**High** — especially on the "Done For You" service. AIWise's entry point ($990 discovery → $3K+ implementation) is a direct parallel to DecisionBetter's discovery call → DFY workflow. AIWise may be faster to close deals due to transparent pricing and productized offerings.

**Key distinction**: DecisionBetter's workshop product (training the client's team, outcome guarantee, "AI coworker" philosophy) has no direct equivalent at AIWise. The philosophies differ: AIWise builds systems FOR clients; DecisionBetter builds capability IN clients.
