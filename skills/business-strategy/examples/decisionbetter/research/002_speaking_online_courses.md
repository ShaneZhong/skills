## 1. Speaking Engagements as Lead Generation

### Does it work for consultants? [Informed opinion — multiple sources]
Speaking at conferences builds authority faster than most marketing channels. It positions consultants above peers and generates inbound leads. Key conditions for ROI:
- Must have structured follow-up (CTA during talk → email list / LinkedIn connection)
- Should target conferences where the AUDIENCE is the target customer (SMB owners, not other consultants)
- Most effective when combined with content publication (talk → blog post → LinkedIn → SEO)

### Relevant AU conferences [Informed opinion — conference sites]
- **AI for Business Summit** (aisummitaustralia.com.au): Dedicated to AI in enterprise. Audience likely large enterprise but could be adapted.
- **NORTH Link AI Summit 2026**: Focuses on "real-world potential and risks of AI for SMEs" — highly aligned audience.
- **AI Leadership Summit** (co-presented by CEDA/NAIC): Government/enterprise focus, less relevant for SMB-focused DecisionBetter.
- **ARA Leaders Forum** (Sydney, retail focus): 100% aligned with one of DecisionBetter's target segments.
- **Innovation conferences (Sydney, March 24–27, 2026)**: Topics include "human and AI teamwork" — relevant.

### Key finding
Speaking is a **high-optionality, low-cost, moderate-effort** lead generation channel. A single talk at the right event (e.g., professional services association conference) can generate 10–20 warm leads. However, the ROI depends heavily on:
1. Being in the room with the right audience (industry association events > general tech conferences)
2. Having a clear offer/CTA to follow up with (productized workshop is the natural CTA)

**Counter-research**: Speaking engagements generate leads but are not scalable by themselves. Most speakers at SMB conferences are not converting talks into meaningful consulting revenue without a strong follow-up system.

## 2. Online Courses / Digital Products

### Market reality [Informed opinion — multiple sources]
- Online course creation ranks #13 passive income idea for 2025
- Successful Udemy course creators earn $2K–$20K+/month
- BUT: "passive" requires significant upfront investment + ongoing updates
- Most digital products "flop" — the market is saturated with generic AI courses
- Best performers are highly specific (solves one problem for one audience) or backed by strong authority

### Service revenue vs. product revenue comparison [Informed opinion]
- AI consulting services: 26.2% CAGR, high margins, recurring demand
- Online courses: lower margins, high competition (Coursera, HBS Online, Udemy all compete), requires audience first
- Key insight from research: **Service revenue is more reliable and higher-value than digital product revenue at this stage**. Successful consultants build courses AFTER establishing service revenue and audience, not instead of it.

### Relevant data point
Only 13% of AI projects move from POC to production (Capgemini). 74% of organisations hope to grow revenue through AI but only 20% are doing so. This structural failure rate INCREASES demand for implementation consulting — it does NOT support DIY course substitutes.

### Assessment for DecisionBetter
Online course is a **Tier 3 / speculative option** at this stage:
- No existing audience (requires content marketing first — Option C — before a course would sell)
- Market is saturated with generic AI courses; differentiation requires AU-specific + industry-specific niche
- Would dilute focus from higher-value service delivery
- Could be considered at iteration 10+ if content marketing (Option C) has built a significant audience
