## Research Hypothesis

I am researching (1) speaking engagements because I need to validate/invalidate the assumption that conference speaking is a viable lead generation channel for an early-stage AI consultant targeting AU SMBs, and (2) online courses/digital products because I need to validate whether a course product is a viable near-term revenue diversification or premature distraction.

**Counter-hypothesis**: Speaking is a vanity channel with poor ROI; online courses require audience that doesn't yet exist.

---

## Research Conducted

**Searches run:**
1. "AI consulting thought leadership speaking engagements Australia business conferences SMB 2025 2026" → [Informed opinion] AI for Business Summit, NORTH Link AI Summit 2026 (explicitly SME-focused), ARA Leaders Forum (retail industry) — all relevant opportunities for Shane to pitch as speaker
2. "AI for business online course productized Australia indie consultant revenue 2025" → [Hard data/Informed] Only 13% of AI projects move from POC to production (Capgemini); service revenue more reliable than course revenue at early stage
3. "speaking at business conferences lead generation consulting ROI does it work" → [Informed opinion] Works when: (a) target audience present, (b) structured CTA + follow-up, (c) combined with content publication
4. "AI consulting online course product passive income realities failures vs service revenue 2025" → [Informed opinion] Most digital products flop; Udemy creators earn $2K–$20K/mo in successful cases; but requires audience first; service revenue is higher-value and more reliable

**Key findings:**
- Speaking at industry association events (accountants, real estate, retail) is HIGH ROI for an AI consultant — the audience is exactly DecisionBetter's target customer and they attend few talks that speak directly to their workflows
- Online course is premature at this stage; needs an audience (Option C) first before the revenue materialises
- Counter-finding: Speaking at AI/tech conferences (vs. industry association events) is LOW ROI for DecisionBetter — preaching to the converted, not reaching SMB buyers

## Key Insight from This Iteration

**Speaking at industry association events (not AI conferences) is an underrated acquisition channel.** The critical distinction is audience type: AI conferences attract practitioners and competitors, not SMB buyers. Industry association events (CPAs, real estate agents, retailers) attract exactly DecisionBetter's target customers who almost never hear a talk that speaks directly to their AI questions in plain language. Shane's communication strength — "translating AI to non-technical audiences" — is the perfect match for this channel. This may be the highest-ROI near-term marketing action available, and it costs nothing but preparation time.
