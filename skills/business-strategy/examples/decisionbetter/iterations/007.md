## Key Findings from Pricing Research

### Finding 1: DecisionBetter is priced BELOW comparable AU market offerings

| Competitor | Comparable product | Their price | DecisionBetter price | Position |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| AI Avenue | Team workshop (5+ people) | From $5,500 | $2,500-$4,500 | 45-80% below |
| PM-Partners | 1-day workshop (generic) | From $795+GST | $4,500 (customised) | Significantly above commodity, well below premium |
| AIWise | Entry discovery session | $990 | $997 | Near parity |

**Conclusion (Hard data)**: DecisionBetter's proposed pricing is not over-market. It is a deliberate market-entry position that is under-market relative to comparable customised offerings. This is appropriate while social proof is being built.

---

### Finding 2: Value-based pricing confirms prices are conservative

| Product | Conservative annual ROI delivered | Price | Value capture % |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI Quick Wins Session ($997) | $26,000 (2 hrs/wk recovered × 5 people × $50/hr) | $997 | 3.8% |
| AI Workflow Sprint ($2,500) | $25,000 (2 workflows × 1 hr/day saved) | $2,500 | 10% |
| AI Team Transformation ($4,500) | $56,250 (3-4 workflows × 1.5 hr/day saved) | $4,500 | 8% |
| AI Adoption Program ($12,000) | $50,000+ (5+ workflows at $10K each) | $12,000 | 24% |

**Conclusion (Informed opinion + first principles)**: Industry standard for consulting value capture is typically 10-20%. DecisionBetter's first two tiers capture less than 10% of delivered value. The pricing is conservative — strong justification for the prices to the client, and room to raise prices as evidence builds.

---

### Finding 3: Psychological price points are correctly engineered

- **$997**: Below the $1,000 "four-figure" threshold. Creates self-approval framing ("under a thousand dollars"). Identical to AIWise's $990, validating the benchmark. Could test $1,197 in Phase 2 (still below $1,200, feels incremental).
- **$2,500**: "First serious commitment" zone. Below $3,000 which triggers harder scrutiny from SMB owners. AI Avenue charges $5,500+ for a comparable team session — significant room to increase.
- **$4,500**: Below the $5,000 threshold where many SMB businesses require additional approvals. Could be raised to $5,500 once 5+ reviews exist.
- **$12,000**: Positions as "transformation investment." Below $15,000 which is the floor of formal procurement for many businesses. Should increase to $15,000-$20,000 at Year 2.

---

### Finding 4: A gap exists between $4,500 and $12,000

The jump from $4,500 to $12,000 is 167% — the largest step in the ladder. This gap may cause clients who complete a $4,500 session to stall before committing to a $12,000 program, even if they want more.

**Proposed addition**: Optional **AI Deep Dive Program** at $7,500 — 3 sessions over 3 weeks, covering 4-5 workflows in depth, team practice between sessions, final review session. This:
- Fills the $4,500-$12,000 gap
- Captures "more than one day, not quite ready for 6 weeks" clients
- Converts more $4,500 customers to higher tiers before the full program

**Updated ladder**:
1. AI Quick Wins Session: $997 (3 hours)
2. AI Workflow Sprint: $2,500 (half-day)
3. AI Team Transformation: $4,500 (full-day + 60-day support)
4. AI Deep Dive Program: $7,500 (3 sessions × ~3 hours over 3 weeks) ← NEW
5. AI Adoption Program: $12,000 (4-6 week transformation)

---

### Finding 5: Price differentiation by segment is not currently explicit

The pricing ladder is one-size-fits-all. In practice, Segment 2 (Operational Leader, $2M-$20M revenue) has higher willingness to pay and can justify premium pricing. Consider:
- **Standard pricing**: Published prices as above (serves Segment 1, 3)
- **Enterprise add-on**: +$1,500 for multi-site, cross-department, or larger team delivery (serves Segment 2)

This is optional and can be implemented later — do not add complexity at launch.

## Pricing Rationale Summary (for owner)

The proposed pricing ($997 / $2,500 / $4,500 / $12,000) is justified by three independent analyses:

1. **Market benchmarking**: All prices are below comparable offerings from the closest AU competitor (AI Avenue at $5,500+). The prices are market-entry penetration prices, not permanent ceilings.

2. **Value-based pricing**: Each tier captures 4-24% of the conservative annual ROI delivered. Standard consulting is 10-20% capture. The lowest two tiers (3.8% and 10%) leave significant value on the table — strong justification for the client, room to grow for DecisionBetter.

3. **Psychological architecture**: Each price point is designed to minimize approval friction for the target segment:
   - $997 = self-approval for business owners
   - $2,500 = within discretionary spend for SMB managers
   - $4,500 = below the $5,000 additional-approval threshold
   - $12,000 = below the $15,000 formal procurement threshold

**The pricing is not arbitrary. It is deliberately conservative as a market-entry strategy.** After 10 client case studies with documented outcomes, prices should increase 20-30% across the board. The outcome guarantee supports premium pricing once evidence exists.

## Key Insight from Iteration 7

The pricing analysis reveals DecisionBetter is leaving money on the table at current prices. This is intentional and correct for a pre-review business building market position. But it also reveals an important sequencing insight: **the urgency of building social proof is financial, not just strategic**. Every week without 3+ published case studies is a week that prices cannot be raised. The social proof sprint (iterations 5-6) is not optional — it is the path to the 20-30% price increase that would materially improve DecisionBetter's revenue per hour of Shane's time.
