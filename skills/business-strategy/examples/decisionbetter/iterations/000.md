## Options Generated

### A. Productized Workshop Ladder (Near-term, 0–6 months)
Define 3 fixed-price workshop products (half-day AUD $2,500; full-day AUD $4,500; multi-session AUD $12,000) with public pricing on the website. This is the core existing offering made explicit and purchasable without a discovery call.

**Why viable now**: AIWise's transparent entry point validates that AU SMBs will transact on visible pricing. Market has no dominant competitor at this specific price/product combination. DecisionBetter's outcome guarantee adds a meaningful trust signal that reduces buyer risk.

**Risks**: Competitor imitation; not all clients fit fixed scope; requires website investment.

**Segments**: 1, 2, 3

### B. Referral Partner Network (Near-term, 0–6 months)
Partner with accountants, bookkeepers, business coaches, and HR consultants in Sydney to generate warm referrals. Offer 10–15% finder's fee or reciprocal referral. Start with 10 partners in the first 90 days.

**Why viable now**: Accounting/bookkeeping industry is the closest trusted advisor to Australian SMB owners; they have regular "should I use AI?" conversations. Low cost, high trust leads.

**Risks**: Slow to build; partners may not actively sell; dependent on Shane's time.

**Segments**: 1, 2

### C. Content-Led Inbound: LinkedIn + SEO (Near-term, 0–6 months, compounding)
Publish 2–3 LinkedIn posts/week targeting SMB owners + 1 SEO blog post/month on "AI for [specific industry]" topics. Goal: become the visible go-to for "AI consulting Sydney/Australia SMB" within 6 months.

**Why viable now**: No competitor owns this content space — Clutch/GoodFirms show low review counts for all AU SMB AI consultants, suggesting none have significant brand presence. Solo operator can punch above their weight with consistent content output (AI tools reduce production cost).

**Risks**: 3–6 month lag; requires consistent execution; content strategy must be narrow enough to rank.

**Segments**: 1, 2, 3

### D. AI Retainer Program — Post-DFY (Strategic bet, 6–24 months)
Design a monthly "AI Optimization Retainer" (AUD $1,500–$3,500/month) for all DFY clients. Covers: ongoing monitoring, workflow expansion, team re-training as tools evolve, priority support.

**Why viable now**: Already delivering informal ongoing support ("no lock-in contracts" implies some ongoing engagement). Formalising this into a priced retainer converts a cost (support time) into a revenue stream. The key insight: clients who've already invested in AI systems have an ongoing maintenance need that isn't being captured.

**Risks**: Requires a larger DFY client base first; churn if perceived value doesn't justify cost.

**Segments**: 2

### E. Industry Vertical Specialisation — Professional Services (Strategic bet, 6–24 months)
Choose professional services (accounting, law, real estate, financial advice) as the primary target vertical. Build 3–5 deep case studies, vertical-specific content, and direct outreach to professional services industry bodies (CPA Australia, Law Institute, REIV). Aim to become "the AI consultant for professional services firms in Sydney."

**Why viable now**: Professional services are among the highest AI-intent industries in Australia (marketing/ICT/consulting at 91%). These firms are also higher-margin clients with clearer ROI from AI automation (billable hour economics make time savings directly visible).

**Risks**: Narrows TAM; wrong vertical choice is slow to reverse; industry associations are slow-moving gatekeepers.

**Segments**: 2, 3

## Key Insight from This Iteration

**The 5% fully-enabled gap is the real strategic opportunity.** 37% of AU SMBs have "adopted" AI but only 5% are fully realising its value — meaning ~32% of all SMBs are technically warm leads who already believe in AI but need better implementation help. This is a far easier sale than convincing skeptics. DecisionBetter should position its workshop/DFY offering not as "introduction to AI" but as "finally get the value from AI you're already trying to use." This reframes the target customer from skeptics (expensive to convert) to frustrated early adopters (much cheaper to convert, higher willingness to pay).
