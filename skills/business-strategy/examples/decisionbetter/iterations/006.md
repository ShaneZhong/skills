## Path A: Build Acquisition System Around Current Model

**Definition**: Keep bespoke workshop + DFY. Build a systematic lead generation engine (referral partners, direct outreach, speaking, network activation) without changing the offer structure.

### WWHTBT for Path A to Be the RIGHT Move

**A1. The current bespoke offer converts at high enough rates that acquiring more leads is the bottleneck.**
- Status: **Unvalidated.** Shane has early clients but no conversion rate on cold/warm leads. Without this data, we cannot confirm the bottleneck is leads rather than offer clarity.
- Evidence needed: Track next 10 warm leads through the current process; measure conversion rate and time-per-lead.

**A2. Shane has sufficient capacity to serve demand generated by an acquisition system WITHOUT productization.**
- Status: **NOT MET.** Current model requires Shane's time for: scoping calls, delivery, follow-up. At 3-4 clients/month capacity, even a modest acquisition system would quickly saturate. An effective referral network generating 6-8 warm leads/month at 30% conversion = 2 new clients/month = near capacity, with each requiring a bespoke scoping call.
- Evidence: Confirmed by the diagnosis — capacity ceiling is the root cause of the scaling problem, not lack of leads.

**A3. The acquisition channels do NOT require a specific product to point prospects toward.**
- Status: **NOT MET.** Every acquisition channel (speaking CTA, referral partner referral, direct outreach) works better with a specific, purchasable product. Without it:
  - Speaking CTA: "Visit my website" → prospect sees bespoke consulting → must book discovery call → long cycle
  - Referral partner: "I know someone who can help" → prospect must go through full scoping → partner can't predict what to refer to
  - Direct outreach: Must still propose custom scope before any commitment → long sales cycle

**A4. The bespoke model's higher per-project revenue justifies the lower conversion efficiency.**
- Status: **Partially validated.** Bespoke DFY ($15K-$50K) is higher value than any workshop product. But: the workshop ladder generates DFY pipeline as upsells — the question is which approach gets to a DFY sale faster. A productized entry ($2,500 workshop) that converts 40% to DFY is more efficient than a direct DFY outreach that requires bespoke scoping at every step.

**Path A Verdict**: 2 of 4 WWHTBT conditions NOT MET. Path A alone is not viable as the starting point. The acquisition system works — but only if something specific exists to point it toward.

## Path B: Productize First

**Definition**: Before activating acquisition channels, redesign offerings as fixed-scope, fixed-price, outcome-led products with visible pricing, online intake, and clear scope definitions. Then point ALL acquisition channels at these specific products.

### WWHTBT for Path B to Be the RIGHT Move

**B1. Fixed-scope products can be designed without sacrificing DecisionBetter's core differentiation (real client data, hands-on delivery, outcome guarantee).**
- Status: **VALIDATED (Iteration 004 WWHTBT).** Diagnostic intake form + outcome-led naming + hybrid qualifying model ($15-min call for $4.5K+ products) maintains differentiation within fixed scope.

**B2. The 2-4 week productization sprint represents acceptable revenue delay.**
- Status: **VALIDATED for a side project context.** For a stealth side project, 2-4 weeks delay is minimal. If Shane is currently generating some revenue from existing clients, this delay has low impact.

**B3. The productized offer will attract buyers once acquisition channels are activated.**
- Status: **VALIDATED by market comps.** AI Avenue at $5,500+ for group workshops has paying clients. DecisionBetter's proposed $2,500 workshop is priced below comparable offerings, with stronger differentiation (outcome guarantee + real client data). Offer-market fit risk is low.

**B4. Productization creates durable leverage: all future acquisition channels benefit permanently from one initial sprint.**
- Status: **VALIDATED by first principles.** A specific, purchasable product:
  - Can be referenced by referral partners without needing to explain the whole business model
  - Gives speaking CTAs a direct booking link ("Book the $997 session here")
  - Allows direct outreach to name a specific offer in the first message
  - Enables SEO around specific, searchable product terms
  - Provides a foundation for AI-delivered training (you cannot automate bespoke consulting)

**Path B Verdict**: All 4 WWHTBT conditions validated. Path B is the right starting point.

## Sequencing Analysis

**Can Path A and Path B run simultaneously?**
- In theory: yes. In practice: no. Running acquisition while building products splits Shane's limited time. Inbound leads generated by acquisition channels hit a bespoke scoping funnel, creating frustration and time drain.
- Exception: warm network activation (direct 1:1 outreach to existing contacts) can start IMMEDIATELY in week 1, before productization is complete. This is the one Path A element that is stealth-compatible and doesn't require a product page to work.

**Recommended Sequence:**

```
Week 1-2:    PARALLEL START
             → Productization: draft 4 product scopes + names + pricing
             → Warm outreach: 10 personal contacts, informal conversations
             → Register: Anthropic Claude Partner Network (30 minutes)

Week 3-4:    PRODUCTIZATION COMPLETE
             → Website: product pages live with prices + intake form + 15-min booking link
             → Social proof sprint begins: actively close first 3 workshop bookings

Week 5-10:   SOCIAL PROOF SPRINT
             → Deliver 3 workshops (potentially at slight discount for case study rights)
             → Document measurable outcomes
             → Get 2-3 Google/Clutch reviews
             → Referral partner conversations: start with 5 accountants/business coaches

Week 8-12:   ACQUISITION CHANNELS ACTIVATED (with social proof)
             → Referral partner network formalized (Option B)
             → Private member event speaking pitches (closed events only — stealth)
             → Direct outreach campaign (targeted by industry/firm size)
             → SEO content publishing (website-only, no social amplification)
```

## Critical Path Insight: Why This Fork Is a False Dilemma

The "Path A vs Path B" framing implies a binary choice. The correct frame is **Path B IS the prerequisite for Path A**. The acquisition system (Path A) requires products to point at. Without Path B, Path A generates leads that fall into a bespoke scoping black hole that wastes more of Shane's time than not having leads at all.

The strategic fork is therefore:
- NOT "acquisition system OR productize" — it is "productize THEN activate acquisition system"
- The REAL fork is: "How fast should we productize, and how minimally?"

**Answer**: The minimum viable productization in 2 weeks is:
1. 3 product names + outcome descriptions (2 hours)
2. 3 price points (1 hour)
3. 3 pages added to website with booking links (5 hours)
4. Diagnostic intake form (2 hours)
Total: ~10 hours. This is the enabler of everything else. There is no argument for delaying this.
