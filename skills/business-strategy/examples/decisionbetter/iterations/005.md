### Challenge 2: Address social proof gap before productizing
**PARTIALLY ACCEPTED with rebuttal.**

The Reviewer argues: productize → social proof. Correct logic, wrong sequencing conclusion. The real insight is that productization ENABLES social proof: a specific product ("AI Workflow Sprint") is easier to get a named review for than "AI consulting services." The resolution:

- Productization sprint: weeks 1–3 (naming, pricing, website update, intake form)

This is faster than "social proof first, then productize" because productization is required to make the case studies referenceable. You cannot send someone to a website product page that doesn't exist.

**Score change**: Option A Feasibility reduced from 5 → 4. Building the pages is trivial (5), but generating demand without social proof is harder (3). Averaged: 4 is appropriate. *Score change reason: acknowledging demand-generation difficulty alongside trivial build effort.*

**ACCEPTED. Clarification required.**

The guarantee is currently vague ("measurable time savings or continued support"). This is insufficient in a disillusionment trough where buyers are skeptical of AI ROI claims.

**Proposed guarantee specification by product tier:**
- **AI Quick Wins Session ($997)**: You will leave with at least 3 specific, implementable AI workflow opportunities identified for your business, or we refund the session fee.

This is the highest-priority constraint change. Shane does not want public-facing content marketing. This eliminates or significantly modifies:
- **Option C (Content-Led Inbound)**: LinkedIn posts are out. SEO blog content could be stealth-compatible (website-only, no social amplification), but the primary acquisition hypothesis for Option C was LinkedIn. Score and tier must be updated.

**Key insight**: Stealth constraint does NOT eliminate content marketing entirely. A website with SEO-optimised content is stealth-compatible because Google indexing is passive — the content exists for searchers, not for a public network audience. The distinction:
- **Not stealth**: LinkedIn posts, Twitter/X, public podcast appearances, conference talks on public programmes
- **Stealth-compatible**: Website SEO content, private referral networks, direct outreach, closed community memberships, private member event speaking

**Score change for Option C**: Impact 4 → 3 (LinkedIn is the primary driver of B2B awareness; SEO-only Content option is slower and weaker), Speed 2 → 1 (SEO-only takes 6-12+ months to generate meaningful traffic), Feasibility 3 → 4 (actually easier without the content production volume LinkedIn demands). New composite: (3×3 + 4×2 + 1×1 + 4×2 + 5×2 + 4×2 + 3×2) / 14 = (9+8+1+8+10+8+6)/14 = 50/14 = **3.57**

*Score change reasons: Impact reduced 4→3 (primary channel LinkedIn eliminated by stealth constraint); Speed reduced 2→1 (SEO-only is very slow); Feasibility raised 3→4 (no LinkedIn posting is actually less work).*

Option C drops below Tier 1 threshold. Move to Tier 2, repositioned as "SEO + private community content" rather than "LinkedIn + SEO."

## Key Insight from Iteration 5

The stealth constraint is the biggest strategic update. It eliminates the primary awareness channel (LinkedIn) and forces the acquisition strategy to rely on:
1. Referral networks (private, relationship-driven)
2. Direct outreach (one-to-one, not broadcast)
3. Association member events (closed, not public)
4. Anthropic partner directory (inbound, passive)
5. SEO (passive, long-term)

This is actually a more robust acquisition system than content marketing — referral and direct channels have higher conversion rates and are less competitive. The constraint reveals a better path.
