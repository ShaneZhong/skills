## Research Hypothesis

I am researching stealth-compatible B2B acquisition channels because the stealth constraint eliminates LinkedIn (the primary channel in Option C) and requires identifying alternative channels that work without public marketing.

**Counter-hypothesis**: Without LinkedIn/social, there are no viable acquisition channels for a solo AI consultant in Australia. Cold outreach doesn't work. Referrals are too slow.

**Counter-research finding**: This is false. Databricks built $100M ARR before public launch. Enterprise B2B consistently demonstrates that direct relationships, referral networks, and targeted outreach outperform content marketing in conversion rate, even if they lose in volume. The constraint is not insurmountable.

See full channel analysis in research/008_stealth_acquisition.md.

## Key Findings

### Finding 1: Stealth constraint reveals a better acquisition stack

The six stealth-compatible channels (warm network, referral partners, direct outreach, private speaking, passive SEO, Anthropic directory) collectively can generate sufficient pipeline without any public content marketing. More importantly: the channels eliminated by stealth (LinkedIn posts, public speaking at conferences) are the lowest-conversion channels anyway. Referral-sourced clients convert at 40-60% vs. 1-2% for content marketing.

### Finding 2: Option C (Content-Led Inbound) needs major revision

Option C was originally designed as "LinkedIn 2-3 posts/week + SEO blog 1 post/month." With LinkedIn posts eliminated:
- The SEO component remains viable (stealth-compatible)
- But SEO alone is a 6-12 month channel — slow, low-volume initially
- Option C must be reconceived as "SEO-Only Content Strategy" — a long-term, passive channel, not a near-term acquisition driver

This changes Option C's role in the funnel from "primary awareness channel" to "long-term passive asset." The Speed score drops from 2 to 1, further cementing its Tier 2 (not Tier 1) status.

### Finding 3: Anthropic Partner Directory timing advantage

The Claude Partner Network launched March 12, 2026 (this week). Registering now means:
- Among the first AU SMB consultants in the directory
- Zero competition in the directory for "AU SMB Claude implementation" — at least for the next 1-3 months
- This is a rare first-mover window that closes quickly as more consultants register

**Action**: Register in the Claude Partner Network immediately (30-minute task). This is the single highest-ROI action relative to time invested in the near term.

### Finding 4: Direct outreach deserves its own option

Previously, cold outreach was implicit in the strategy but not formalized. Given stealth constraints, direct outreach becomes one of the primary acquisition channels. It warrants a dedicated option.

## New Option: Option O — Targeted Direct Outreach Campaign
**What**: A systematic, time-boxed program of personalised direct outreach to 400-500 specific prospects over 8 weeks. Channels: personalised cold email (via Apollo.io or LinkedIn DMs — private messages only, no posts). Target: Operations Managers, GMs, and business owners in Segments 2 and 3 (20-100 employee professional services firms in Sydney). One specific product mentioned in outreach: the $2,500 AI Workflow Sprint as the entry offer.

**Why it works without public presence**:
- Completely private — 1:1 messages leave no public trace
- Highly targeted: no need to broadcast to thousands when 400 personalised messages can generate the same pipeline
- Specific product mention increases response rate (not "let's have a chat" but "I'd like to show you what AI did for another accounting firm in 45 minutes")

**Differentiated from Option N (Warm Network)**: Option N is Shane's existing personal network (warm). Option O is systematic cold outreach to new prospects (cold). Both are stealth-compatible; they serve different pipeline phases.

**Scope**:
- Week 1-2: Build target list (100 companies in 2-3 industries in Sydney, with relevant LinkedIn/email data)
- Week 3-10: 10 personalised outreaches/day, 5 days/week = 400 total
- Template: "I noticed [specific trigger] at [Company]. We recently helped [similar firm] save [X hours/week] using AI in their [workflow]. Happy to show you the same approach in 45 minutes."
- CTA: Link to the $2,500 AI Workflow Sprint product page

**Volume estimates**:
- 400 outreaches × 5% response rate = 20 conversations
- 20 conversations × 35% conversion = 7 workshop bookings over 8 weeks
- Average value $3,000 → $21,000 additional revenue in the 8-week window

**Tags**: Near-term (0-3 months) | Segments: 2, 3

## Key Insight from Iteration 8

The stealth constraint creates a paradox: by eliminating the lowest-friction, highest-noise channels (LinkedIn), it forces investment in the highest-quality, relationship-driven channels (warm network, referral partners, direct outreach). These channels have higher conversion rates (40-60% for referrals vs. 1-2% for content marketing) but require more intentional effort per lead. The result: fewer total leads but far better pipeline quality. For a solo founder with capacity constraints, this is actually superior to a high-volume content-driven approach that would saturate capacity with poorly-qualified leads.
