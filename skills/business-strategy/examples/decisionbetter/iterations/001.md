## Research Hypothesis

I am researching (1) government grant programs because I need to validate/invalidate the assumption that DecisionBetter could access government-funded client flow, and (2) cohort/group workshop models because I need to validate whether there is a scalable group delivery format that preserves DecisionBetter's "real client data" differentiation.

**Counter-hypothesis**: Government programs are bureaucratic and slow; cohort models sacrifice the personalisation that is DecisionBetter's core differentiation.

---

## Research Conducted

**Searches run:**
1. "AI consulting group programs cohort workshop business model Australia 2025" → [Informed opinion] AI Consulting Group (1–3 day org workshops), Matrix AI (group settings), various cohort programs exist; cohort fatigue is a known risk
2. "white label AI consulting reseller partner program Australia 2025" → [Informed opinion] White-label AI platforms exist (CustomGPT.ai, GoHighLevel) — not a strong AU-specific angle
3. "Australian government AI grants small business 2025 2026 digital transformation funding" → [Hard data] AI Adopt Program ($3M–$5M per centre), ASBAS Digital Solutions Round 3 ($25.1M over 5 years), SA AI Capability Pilot ($370K over 18 months)
4. "ASBAS Digital Solutions Round 3 registered service provider how to apply consulting 2025" → [Hard data] Applications closed 5 December 2025 — this round is past; eligible applicants must be incorporated entities (solo operator may not qualify directly)
5. "ASBAS Digital Solutions program problems complaints delivery failures slow payment" → [Anecdotal] Limited complaints found; one indication that at least one provider stopped delivery without explanation; general bureaucratic overhead concern

**Key findings:**
- Government grants are largely inaccessible directly to a solo/early-stage operator. Best angle: **become a subcontractor/delivery partner to existing ASBAS or AI Adopt Centre grantees** who need specialist AI workshop content delivery
- Cohort models offer significant revenue multiplication (8 clients × $3K = $24K for same delivery effort as 1:1), but risk diluting the "real client data" differentiation
- Industry-specific cohorts could preserve differentiation while enabling scale

### Option G: Industry-Specific Cohort Program
- **What**: Design and run a 6-week cohort-based group program for 6–8 SMB owners from the same industry (e.g., "AI for Accountants" or "AI for Real Estate Principals"). Each participant brings their own business problems; the program covers AI foundations + 3–4 workflow automation implementations per participant, with peer learning across the cohort. Price: AUD $3,500–$5,000 per participant.
- **Why it might work**: Revenue multiplication: 8 × $4,000 = $32,000 for ~15 hours of facilitation (vs. $5,000 for a full-day solo workshop). Peer learning enhances outcomes. Industry-specific cohort means common AI use cases, shared vocabulary, and strong referral dynamics within the industry. No custom scoping required per participant if industry is chosen well.
- **Tags**: Strategic bet (6–12 months — needs 2–3 pilots first) | Segments: 2, 3
- **Obvious risks**: Cohort filling logistics (requires minimum 6 participants before launching); industry-specific positioning conflicts with generalist current approach; "group" feel may dilute the "hands-on with your data" differentiation; scheduling 8 SMB owners for 6 weeks is operationally complex

## Key Insight from This Iteration

**Government grants are a pipeline, not a revenue source.** The ASBAS program is well-funded ($25M) but the application window just closed. The most actionable government angle is becoming a subcontractor to an existing ASBAS grantee — this requires outreach now while grantees are planning their delivery. However, government subcontracting is slow and margin-compressed. The cohort model (Option G) is more immediately actionable and addresses the capacity constraint more directly than government partnerships.

**The real unlock**: Combining Option A (productized workshop) with Option G (cohort) creates a **workshop funnel**: individual workshops identify which clients have enough workflow complexity to warrant cohort participation or DFY. This sequential funnel thinking will be explored in later iterations.
